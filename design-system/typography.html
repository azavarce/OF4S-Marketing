03 · Foundation

Editorial weight, working clarity.

Two families. Schibsted Grotesk — a characterful grotesk — for headlines, hero copy, and the wordmark itself. Geist — a clean grotesque — for everything else: UI, body, captions, tables. Together they look unmistakably designed without trying too hard.

3.1

Why this pairing

Editorial > Corporate. Most dealers default to Arial / Helvetica / Inter. We pick a serif that gives presentations gravity and a sans that quietly does the work.

REASON / 01

It separates us from the catalog look.

JC White, Pradere, and Office Furniture Warehouse all live in geometric sans-serif land. A modern serif headline immediately reads premium and considered without resorting to luxury-brand cliché.

REASON / 02

The accent word gives the brand a voice.

One word per headline, set in accent navy — the emotional bit ("made easy"). The brand already lives in the wordmark; the accent word carries it into every layout.

REASON / 03

Geist is built for screens.

Open apertures, generous spacing, and a wide weight range mean it stays sharp at 12px on a quote PDF and at 96px on a hero. We don't need a separate UI font.

REASON / 04

Free, open, and on Google Fonts.

Both families are open-source and licensed for commercial use without fees. Easy to embed on the website and into PowerPoint/Keynote/Google Slides without licensing friction.

CASE STUDY · 2026 · MIRAMAR

A 14,000 sq ft buildout, specified, ordered, and installed in nine weeks.

When a fast-growing logistics firm needed to be in their new Miramar HQ before Q2, the legacy dealers quoted a 16-week lead. We delivered in 9.

We started with a Friday afternoon walk-through and a Monday morning quote. By Wednesday the client had digital renderings of every workspace, configurable in real time. Six weeks later the trucks rolled in. The project came in 4% under budget — and the team moved into a space that actually felt like theirs, not a catalog spread.
