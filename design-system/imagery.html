5.3

Track C — The duotone treatment

A signature marketing finish. Any photo, processed to a navy duotone, immediately reads as ours. Used sparingly: hero overlays, social posts, and section dividers in decks.

Original · full color
→
Duotone · navy 800 + navy 300
RECIPE
  1. Start with a high-contrast B&W version of the source photo.
  2. Map shadows to Navy 800 (#15273F).
  3. Map highlights to Navy 300 (#8FA9C7).
  4. Keep highlights cool — the light navy should read airy, not gray.
  5. Keep midtone contrast strong — duotones lose impact when flat.

5.5

Composition cheat-sheet

A few rules that make photography feel like ours regardless of who's behind the camera.

RULE 01

Eye-level, never god's-eye

Stand where a person stands. Avoid drone shots and ceiling-mounted angles unless documenting a ceiling.

RULE 02

Morning or afternoon light

Schedule shoots for the first or last 2 hours of daylight. Florida overhead light flattens everything.

RULE 03

One subject per frame

Wide install OR product detail OR a person. Don't try to show everything in one shot — that's catalog energy.

RULE 04

Leave breathing room

Crop with type in mind. Heroes need a quiet third where the headline can land without overlay tricks.
